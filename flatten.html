  • In 2012, the city of Lanzhou plans to flatten 700 mountains on its outskirts. Slice the tops off the mountains, fill in the valleys, and presto: nice, flat space. The new plains will be en route to the airport. Sweltering sun en route to the desert bakes the soil.


  • In Chinese, hill and mountain are the same word: 山. If it’s any consolation, the mountains in Lanzhou might more readily be classified as hills. Semantics aside, it is neither cheap nor easy. Costing about 20 million yuan, work was halted in April 2013, after visible air pollution led to calls for an environmental assessment. While work began again four weeks later due to financial pressure from the local government and contractors, the environmental assessment has yet to be completed. “There has been too little modeling of the costs and benefits of land creation.” “Inexperience and technical problems delay projects and add costs, and the environmental impacts are not being thoroughly considered.” In cities such as Chongqing, Shiyan, Yichang, and now Lanzhou, tens of square kilometres of flatlands have now been created from hills.
